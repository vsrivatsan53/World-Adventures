Tower of London

One of London's most famous landmarks, the historic Tower houses the Crown Jewels, the prison cell of Sir Walter Raleigh, known as the Bloody Tower, and the Chapel of St. John and the Royal Armories.

Hours: Sun - Mon 10:00 am - 4:30 pm Tue - Sat 9:00 am - 4:30 pm

Cost: Adult: £19.00 (28.73 US Dollars)

Child: £9.50 (14.37 US Dollars)

Address: London EC3N 4AB, United Kingdom

Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace serves as both the office and London residence of Her Majesty The Queen. It is one of the few working royal palaces remaining in the world today. During a visit to Buckingham Palace, visitors can see the nineteen magnificent State Rooms, which provide the setting for ceremonial occasions and official entertaining. All rooms are furnished with many of the greatest treasures from the Royal Collection. A visit also includes entry to the special exhibition, Royal Childhood.

Hours: 9:30AM - 7:30 PM

Prices Starting From:

Adult: £20.50 (31 US Dollars)

Over 60/Student (with valid ID): £18.80 (28.43 US Dollars)

Under 17/Disabled: £11.80 (17.85 US Dollars)

Under 5: Free

Family: £52.80 (2 adults and 3 under 17s) (79.85 US Dollars)

Address: London SW1A 1AA, United Kingdom

The National Gallery

The National Gallery houses the national collection of paintings in the Western European tradition from the 13th to the 19th centuries. It is one of the largest art museums and is world renowned for its rare pieces and wide variety. It is on show 361 days a year, free of charge.

Hours: 10:00 am to 6:00 pm

Cost: Free!

Address: Trafalgar Square, London WC2N 5DN, United Kingdom
