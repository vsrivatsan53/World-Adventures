Wat Arun

The Magnificent Wat Arun or Temple of the Sun was constructed during the first half of the 19th century in the ancient Khmer style, the stupa showcasing ornate floral pattern decked out in glazed porcelain is stunning up close. Apart from its beauty, Wat Arun symbolizes the birth of the Rattanakosin Period and the founding of the new capital after Ayutthaya fell. This is a must see for all visiting the magnificent city. This attraction is open daily from 8:30 A.M. – 5:30 P.M and costs a mere 30 Bhat for tourists.

Address: Arun, Bangkok Yai, Bangkok 10600, Thailand

Floating Market

The pioneer of all floating markets, Damnoen Saduak continues to offer an authentic experience despite its increasingly touristy atmosphere. Imagine dozens of wooden row boats floating by, each laden to the brim with farm-fresh fruits, vegetables or flowers. Food vendors fill their vessels with cauldrons and charcoal grills, ready to whip up a bowl of ‘boat noodle’ or seafood skewers upon request.

Wat Pho

One of the oldest and largest temples in Bangkok with numerous beautiful structures such as the famous Reclining Buddha, which is the largest in Thailand measuring more than 150 feet in length. The beautiful temple is revered by many and is a must see for all visiting this City. The hours are 8:00 am to 6:00 pm and admission is 100 bhat.

Address: 2 Sanamchai Road, Grand Palace Subdistrict, Pranakorn District, Bangkok 10200

Baan Dinso 2

Baan Dinso 2 is located at Ratchadamnoen square next to Dinso road, leading to the Giant Swing and Bangkok City Hall. Many infrastructures in this area were built since 1937 and still preserve its original architecture style to current day. Ratchadamnoen is home to several Bangkok’s tourist destination including Rattanakosin Exhibition Hall, Wat Ratchanadda, Wat Thepthidaram, Mahakan Fort, the Golden Mountain and Baan Bat neighborhood. Glance through the old neighborhood and lay eyes upon Ratchadamnoen road, a symbolic path to Siamese Democracy.

Generations of Thai descendants have ventured this road to establish just governance for the public opinion. The Democracy Monument was erected on this foundation of governance and sacrifices made by pioneering individuals. Although time has moved forward, the Democracy Monument stands tall to inscribe memories of the past. We invite you to be our guest to witness Bangkok’s most memorable landscape at an unbelievable location next to the Democracy Monument and Ratchadamnoen road.
